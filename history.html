The African Student Association (ASA) was founded in 2012 to represent the African student population on campus. Our goal is to promote African culture, educate the public, and create a more culturally diverse community through events and programming.

Founding

2012

A group of African students on campus came together to form ASA. They saw a need for an organization that could represent and celebrate the diversity of African cultures on campus.

Early Years

2013-2017

ASA focused on building its membership and establishing itself as a cultural organization on campus. We held events such as cultural performances, discussions, and film screenings to promote awareness of African culture.

African Dinners

2018

ASA began hosting our annual African Dinner event. The event is based on a different theme each year, and features a fashion show, games, music, and other performances. Our aim is to provide an opportunity for people of all backgrounds to come together and learn about African culture.

Other Events

2019-Present

In addition to the African Dinner, ASA hosts a variety of other events throughout the year. These events range from social gatherings and cultural celebrations to educational workshops and community service projects.

Impact

Present

Over the years, ASA has had a significant impact on the campus community. Our events have helped to promote cultural awareness and understanding, and have provided a platform for African students to connect and celebrate their heritage.

Looking Forward

Future

Moving forward, ASA is committed to continuing our mission of promoting African culture and creating a more culturally diverse community. We will continue to host events and programming that educate, inspire, and celebrate the rich diversity of African cultures.

We invite you to join us in celebrating African culture and promoting diversity on campus. Whether you're a student, faculty member, or community member, there are many ways to get involved with ASA. Visit our website to learn more about our upcoming events and how you can become a member.
